Visualising and Analysing Global Connectivity

How do we Connect?

Communication tools throughout the times
From talking drums, cave paintings and travelling poets on horseback in Africa; to smoke signals in the Americas and parts of Asia; to homing pigeons in Persia and later on by the Romans; to the Greek hydraulic semaphore system in Europe in the 4th century, an early attempt at telegraphs; to finally the Electrical Telegraph in the 19th Century. People around the world have always had a need to communicate and connect. Whether to share literature and art or to aide military advancement, people throughout the times have found innovative tools and ways of communicating.

Post Covid

Implications
The existing Digital Divide between those who have access to the required infrastructure and/or devices either because of location or lack of funds became more obvious during the Covid Pandemic, causing more people to be excluded from work and education opportunities or access to healthcare because of their age, disability or socioeconomic status.
